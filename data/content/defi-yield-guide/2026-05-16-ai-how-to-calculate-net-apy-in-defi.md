# How to Calculate Net APY in DeFi

This guide walks you through the exact process of converting raw DeFi returns—including lending interest, reward tokens, fees, and impermanent loss—into a single **net APY** figure you can compare across protocols. By the end, you’ll have a repeatable spreadsheet workflow that accounts for compounding, gas costs, and price volatility, using real‑world numbers from protocols such as Aave V3, SushiSwap, and Uniswap V3 as of early 2025.

---

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### 1. List Every Income Stream for Your Position
Before touching a calculator, enumerate all cash flows generated by your DeFi interaction. Typical streams include:

- **Supply / lending interest** (e.g., 4.2 % APY on USDC in Aave V3)
- **Reward tokens** (e.g., 0.018 AAVE per day on the same USDC position)
- **LP trading fees** (e.g., 0.30 % of swap volume on SushiSwap ETH/USDC)
- **Governance token airdrops** (e.g., 150 SUSHI distributed over 30 days)

Write each stream in a table with columns: *Stream Name, Principal (USD), Daily Rate / Amount, Current Price (USD), and Frequency*.

### 2. Pull Raw Data from the Protocol Dashboard and Block Explorers
- **Protocol UI**: Most platforms (Aave, Compound, SushiSwap) show a *current APY* and a *reward rate* in percentages. Record those percentages directly.
- **Etherscan / Polygonscan**: For on‑chain verification, locate the *Mint* (interest) and *Transfer* (reward) events for your wallet address. Query a 30‑day window ending **28 Feb 2025** to match the example dates.
- **Token Prices**: Use a reliable source such as CoinGecko (price at 00:00 UTC on 28 Feb 2025). For the example, AAVE was $253.40, SUSHI $2.18.

### 3. Compute the **Gross Annual Yield** for Each Stream
Convert daily rates to annual figures assuming simple compounding (you will later adjust for true compounding).

| Stream | Daily Rate (decimal) | Annual Rate (simple) | USD Value (annual) |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| USDC lending interest | 0.000115 (4.2 % APY) | 0.0420 | 0.0420 × $10,000 = $420 |
| AAVE reward | 0.018 AAVE/day | 0.018 × 365 = 6.57 AAVE | 6.57 × $253.40 = $1,665.78 |
| SUSHI LP fees | 0.003 SUSHI/day | 0.003 × 365 = 1.095 SUSHI | 1.095 × $2.18 = $2.39 |

**Formula for each stream**:  
`Annual USD = Principal × (Rate% / 100)` for interest, and  
`Annual USD = (Amount per day × 365) × Current Token Price` for token rewards.

### 4. Subtract All Associated Costs
Costs that directly reduce your return must be converted to an annual USD figure and subtracted from the gross yield.

- **Protocol fees**: Most lending protocols charge a 0.00025 % *governance fee* on interest. In our example:  
  `$420 × 0.00025 = $0.105` per year.
- **Gas / network fees**: If you harvest rewards weekly, estimate the total gas spent in a year. For Ethereum mainnet, a typical weekly harvest cost in early 2025 is **$15** (≈ 0.005 ETH at $3,000/ETH). Over 52 weeks: `$15 × 52 = $780`.
- **Slippage & swap fees**: When converting reward tokens to stablecoins, assume a 0.5 % loss (0.3 % DEX fee + 0.2 % slippage). For the $1,665.78 AAVE reward: `$1,665.78 × 0.005 = $8.33`.

**Total costs**: `$0.105 + $780 + $8.33 = $788.44`.

### 5. Adjust for Impermanent Loss (If You’re an LP)
When providing liquidity on an AMM, you experience impermanent loss (IL). Use the standard IL formula for a 50/50 pool:

`IL = 2 × √(price ratio) / (1 + price ratio) – 1`.
