# DeFi Liquidity Pools Explained

Liquidity pools are smart contracts that lock pairs of tokens to enable automated, decentralized trading without order books, serving as the backbone of decentralized exchanges (DEXs) such as Uniswap, SushiSwap, and Curve. By providing assets to a pool, users become liquidity providers (LPs) and earn a share of trading fees and, often, additional token incentives. Pools use algorithmic market‑making formulas (AMMs) to price assets based on supply ratios, eliminating the need for centralized market makers. The model powers billions of dollars in daily volume; as of early 2025, the DeFi ecosystem holds over **$236 B in total value locked (TVL)** across thousands of pools (DeFi Llama, 2025).

---

## What is a liquidity pool in DeFi?

A liquidity pool is a collection of funds locked in a smart contract that supplies the capital needed for traders to swap one token for another. Each pool typically contains two (or more) token types in a predetermined ratio, and anyone can deposit or withdraw liquidity. The pool’s balance is expressed as a mathematical invariant that governs how trades are executed, ensuring that the total value of the tokens remains constant after each swap. Popular implementations include Uniswap V2 (constant‑product x·y=k), Curve Finance (stable‑swap), and Balancer (weighted pools). As of early 2025, Uniswap V3 alone holds **≈ $8.4 B TVL** across its pools (Dune Analytics, 2025).

## How do liquidity providers earn rewards?

Liquidity providers earn a share of the trading fees generated by the pool—typically **0.03 %–0.30 % per swap**—plus, in many cases, additional governance‑token incentives from liquidity‑mining programs.
