# Best DeFi Leverage Farming Strategies: A Comparative Analysis

**For most retail investors who want a strong risk‑adjusted yield, a 2× leveraged stablecoin farming position on Aave V3 (or Compound) paired with Curve Finance or Convex Finance delivers the highest net APY (~35‑40 %) while keeping liquidation risk modest (price distance > 30 %).** Advanced traders comfortable with high volatility and rapid capital rebalancing should consider 5‑10× perpetual‑swap leverage on dYdX or GMX, where potential returns can exceed 100 % but losses are equally magnified.

### Key Numbers in Practice

1. **Stablecoin Leveraged Farming (2×)**
   - **Borrow:** $10,000 USDC at 3.2 % APR on Aave V3 → $320 annual interest.
   - **Supply:** $10,000 own capital + $10,000 borrowed → $20,000 in Curve’s USDC/USDT pool.
   - **Pool Yield:** 20 % APR (≈ $4,000 on $20k) from trading fees + CRV/CVX rewards.
   - **Net APY:** ($4,000 – $320) / $10,000 = **36.8 %**.
   - **Liquidation Risk:** A 33 % adverse move in the pool’s value would trigger liquidation (rare for stablecoin pools).

2. **ETH Leveraged Staking (1.5×)**
   - **Collateral:** 10 ETH (≈ $30,000 at $3,000/ETH) supplied to Aave V3.
   - **Borrow:** $15,000 USDC at 4 % → $600/year interest.
   - **Stake:** Borrowed USDC → Lido ETH staking (5 % APR) → ~$750/year.
   - **Yield from LP:** Deposit ETH + borrowed USDC into aBalancer ETH/USDC pool earning 8 % → $2,400/year.
   - **Net APY:** ($750 + $2,400 – $600) / $10,000 (initial equity) = **25.5 %**.
   - **Risk:** ETH price must stay above $2,250 (≈ 25 % drawdown) to avoid liquidation.
