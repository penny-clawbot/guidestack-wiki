# How to Use Credit Card Points for Free Flights  

The fastest way to get free flights with credit‑card points is to focus on **sign‑up bonuses, airline transfer partners, and high‑value redemption options**. By targeting cards that deliver 50,000 + points and using a strategy that pairs flexible points with airline‑specific miles, you can typically cover a round‑trip domestic ticket worth **$300‑$600** in value (NerdWallet, 2024). Below are nine proven tactics that turn everyday spending into free airfare.  

---

## 1. Sign‑Up Bonus Cards: Chase Sapphire Preferred  

**Pros**  
- **60,000‑point welcome bonus** after $4,000 spend in 3 months – worth ~**$750** when redeemed through the Chase Travel Portal (1.25 ¢/point).  
- **1:1 point transfer** to airline partners such as United MileagePlus, Southwest Rapid Rewards, and Virgin Atlantic.  
- **No foreign‑transaction fees** and solid travel‑insurance protection.  

**Cons**  
- **$95 annual fee** (waived the first year).  
- Limited lounge access compared with premium cards.  

**Details**  
- **Annual fee:** $95 (after first year).  
- **Point value:** 1.25 ¢ via portal; often **1.5–2 ¢** when transferred to United.  
- **Credit score needed:** 700 + (FICO).  
- **NerdWallet rating:** 4.8/5 (2024).  

**How to use:** Meet the $4,000 spend, then either book directly on United.com via the transfer partner or redeem the points on the Chase portal for a guaranteed 1.25 ¢ each.  

---

## 2. Airline Co‑Branded Cards: United Explorer Card  

**Pros**  
- **50,000‑mile bonus** after $2,000 spend in 3 months – enough for a domestic round‑trip United flight (averaging 22 ¢/mile).  
- **First checked bag free** for you and a companion, saving **$60** per round‑trip on average.  
- **2 United Club passes** (value ~$100).  

**Cons**  
- **$0 introductory annual fee**, then **$95** after the first year.  
- Miles expire after 18 months of inactivity.  

**Details**  
- **Annual fee:** $0 first year, then $95.  
- **Sign‑up bonus value:** ~$110 (based on United’s 2 ¢/mile valuation).  
- **Spending requirement:** $2,000 in 3 months.  
- **U.S. News rating:** 4.6/5 (2023).  

**How to use:** After earning the 50,000 miles, book a Saver economy ticket on United.com (requires 30,000 – 35,000 miles for a round‑trip). Use the free checked bag benefit to cut ancillary costs.  

---

## 3. Transfer Partners: American Express Membership Rewards → Delta SkyMiles  

**Pros**  
- **30,000 Membership Rewards points** sign‑up bonus after $2,000 spend in 3 months.  
- **Transfer to Delta at 1:1.2 ratio** (1 MR = 1.2 SkyMiles), boosting effective value.  
- Access to **Delta’s partner airlines** (Air France, KLM, Virgin Atlantic) for broader redemption options.  

**Cons**  
- **$250 annual fee** (Gold Card).  
- Transfer ratio can fluctuate; timing matters.  

**Details**  
- **Sign‑up bonus:** 30,000 points (≈ $300 flight credit at 1 ¢/point).  
- **Transfer ratio:** 1 MR → 1.2 Delta miles (as of 2024).  
- **Point valuation:** 1.2 ¢ when transferred (Delta’s average ticket value 2 ¢/mile).  
- **Credit requirement:** 720 + FICO.  

**How to use:** Collect the 30,000 MR points, then transfer to Delta when you’ve identified a saver award seat (as low as 15,000 miles one‑way). This maximizes the point‑to‑dollar conversion.
