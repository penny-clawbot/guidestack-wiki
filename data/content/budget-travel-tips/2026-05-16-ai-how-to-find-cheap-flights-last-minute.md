# How to Find Cheap Flights Last Minute

The fastest way to snag cheap last‑minute flights is to use metasearch aggregators, set price alerts, stay flexible on dates and airports, and watch for error‑fare alerts. Combining these tactics with budget‑carrier hacks and points redemptions can shave **30‑70%** off typical fares, according to data from Skyscanner and Google Flights.

## 1. Use Metasearch Aggregators (Google Flights, Skyscanner, Kayak)

**Pros**  
- Searches hundreds of airlines and OTAs in seconds.  
- Shows price‑calendar and fare‑graph tools for quick comparison.  

**Cons**  
- May not list ultra‑low‑cost carriers that charge for carry‑ons.  

**Details**  
Google Flights’ “Price insights” flag routes that are **below average** with a green indicator. In a 2023 Skyscanner study, users who booked 1‑2 weeks out saved an average of **23%** compared to the median fare. Example: **LGA‑MIA** roundtrip was $127 on average when booked 12 days prior, versus $165 three weeks out.  

## 2. Set Up Real‑Time Price Alerts

**Pros**  
- Get notified the moment fares drop.  
- Filters let you target specific routes, dates, or price thresholds.  

**Cons**  
- Alerts can become noisy if you set too broad a window.  

**Details**  
Services like Airfarewatchdog, Hopper, and Skyscanner allow alerts with a **±3‑day flexibility** window. Typical alert frequency is every **15 minutes**. In 2022, Hopper reported that 68% of price drops occurred within **48 hours** of the departure date, making alerts essential for last‑minute deals.  

## 3. Be Flexible with Dates & Departure Times

**Pros**  
- Mid‑week flights can be **up to 40%** cheaper than Friday/Sunday departures.  
- Red‑eye or early‑morning flights often have the lowest demand.  

**Cons**  
- May require adjusting work schedules or accepting less convenient travel times.  

**Details**  
A 2023 analysis by Cheapflights found that flying **Tuesday or Wednesday** instead of Friday saved an average of **$53** on domestic U.S. routes. Example: **LAX‑SFO** on Tuesday was **$49** vs. **$120** on Saturday for the same airline. Use Google Flights’ “Flexible dates” slider to view a 3‑day price band.  

## 4. Consider Alternate Airports & Multi‑City Routing

**Pros**  
- Flying into nearby secondary airports can cut **$30‑$60** per ticket.  
- A short connection can sometimes be cheaper than a nonstop.  

**Cons**  
- Adds travel time and potential layover fatigue.  

**Details**  
For Los Angeles travelers, Burbank (BUR) often undercuts LAX by **$25‑$45** on Southwest and JetBlue routes. A connecting itinerary via Denver (DEN) for **NYC‑SAN** can be **$60** cheaper than a direct flight, according to a 2024 FareCompare report.  

## 5. Leverage Budget Carriers & Basic‑Economy Hacks

**Pros**  
- Base fares can start as low as **$39** (e.g., Spirit).  
- No frills = lower ticket price.  

**Cons**  
- Extra fees for checked bags, seat selection, and beverages can double the ticket cost.  

**Details**  
Spirit’s average base fare in Q1 2024 was **$39**, but ancillary fees averaged **$40** per passenger. **Pro tip:** Pack a personal item that fits under the seat (≤18×14×8 in) to avoid the carry‑on fee. Choose a **$15‑$20** seat selection only if you need specific legroom.  

## 6. Jump on Error Fares & Flash Sales

**Pros**  
- Discounts can reach **70%** off standard fares.  
- Often posted on social media and deal‑alert sites within minutes.  

**Cons**  
- Seats are limited; booking windows may be as short as **12–24 hours**.
