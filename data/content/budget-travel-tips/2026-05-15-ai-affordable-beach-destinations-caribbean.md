# Affordable Beach Destinations in the Caribbean: A Step‑by‑Step Guide

This guide delivers a concrete, money‑saving roadmap for visiting the Caribbean’s best budget beach spots in 2024, with exact flight price windows, island‑by‑island cost breakdowns, and daily budget tactics that can cut your total trip cost by up to **40 %** compared with high‑season travel. By following the eight numbered steps below you’ll secure round‑trip flights for **$300 – $480**, find nightly stays under **$90**, and keep daily food and activity expenses around **$50 – $70**.

---

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Target the Cheapest Travel Window  

The Caribbean’s “low season” runs **June – November**, but hurricane activity peaks in **September**. To balance low prices and safer weather, aim for **early June or late November**.  

- **Flight savings**: Round‑trip economy fares from New York (JFK) to Punta Cana drop to **$310** in early June versus **$520** in December (Skyscanner, 2023 Price Index).  
- **Accommodation discounts**: Hotels lower nightly rates by **15‑25 %** during these months; a 3‑star beachfront property in Montego Bay can be **$68/night** in June vs. **$92/night** in February.  

**Pro tip**: Set a Google Calendar reminder for the first week of June and the last week of November to start your search.

### Step 2: Use Flight‑Price Aggregators & Set Price Alerts  

1. **Google Flights** – Filter by “Best” view to see the cheapest days to fly.  
2. **Skyscanner** – “Whole month” view highlights the lowest‑price dates.  
3. **Kayak** – “Price Forecast” predicts if fares will rise or fall.  

- **Timing**: Book **2 – 3 months** ahead for domestic‑to‑Caribbean routes. Airlines (e.g., JetBlue, Spirit) release flash sales on **Tuesdays at 3 p.m. ET**; this timing historically yields the lowest fares (CheapAir, 2023).  
- **Alerts**: Create alerts for your target route and **departure dates** (e.g., June 10‑15). Most platforms allow you to set a “price cap” (e.g., $350). When fares dip below the cap, you receive an email.
