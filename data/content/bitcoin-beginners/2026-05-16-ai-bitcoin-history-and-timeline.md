# Bitcoin History and Timeline: A Comparative Guide  

**For beginners who just need a quick reference, the Snapshot Timeline is best; for investors and researchers who want deep context, the Detailed Narrative is best.**  

---

## 1. Quick Snapshot Timeline  

- **2009 – Bitcoin Genesis:** First block (block 0) mined on Jan 3 2009. Block reward: **50 BTC**.  
- **2010 – First Real‑World Purchase:** Laszlo Hanyecz bought two pizzas for 10,000 BTC (≈$41).  
- **2011 – Price Parity:** Bitcoin reached **$1.00** (April).  
- **2013 – First Major Bull Run:** Price hit **$1,000** in early November, then fell to ~$300 by December.  
- **2014 – Mt. Gox Collapse:** ~850,000 BTC stolen; market trust shaken.  
- **2015 – BitLicense Introduced:** New York’s regulatory framework for crypto firms.  
- **2016 – Second Halving:** Block reward reduced to **12.5 BTC** (July).  
- **2017 – All‑Time High (ATH) $20,089:** December 17, driven by ICO boom.  
- **2018 – Market Correction:** Price dropped to ~$3,200 by December.  
- **2019 – Institutional Interest:** Bakkt launch, Fidelity Digital Assets.  
- **2020 – Third Halving:** Reward fell to **6.25 BTC** (May).  
- **2021 – New ATH $68,991:** November 10. Institutional adoption (Tesla, MicroStrategy).  
- **2022 – Regulatory Crackdown:** China banned mining, U.S. infrastructure bill taxed crypto.  
- **2023 – Price Recovery:** Bitcoin rebounded to **$30,000** after FTX collapse.  
- **2024 – Fourth Halving:** Reward becomes **3.125 BTC** (April). Price topped **$73,000** in March (CoinMarketCap).

## 2. Detailed Narrative Timeline  

### 2.1 2009–2012: The Experimental Phase  
Bitcoin’s whitepaper, authored by **Satoshi Nakamoto**, was published on Oct 31 2008. The first block (block 0) was mined on Jan 3 2009, creating 50 BTC. Early adopters were mainly cryptographers on the P2P Foundation forum. By 2010, the first exchange (BitcoinMarket.com) listed Bitcoin at $0.003/BTC. The pivotal pizza transaction valued each BTC at $0.0041.  

### 2.2 2013–2015: First Mainstream Wave  
2013 saw rapid price appreciation: Bitcoin crossed $100 in early March, $500 in November, and briefly hit $1,000 on November 5. The rise was fueled by media coverage and the Silk Road shutdown (Oct 2013). However, a series of exchange hacks (including the $460 M Mt. Gox collapse in 2014) caused a 70 % price correction.  

Regulatory steps began: the U.S. Senate held hearings in 2013; by 2015 New York introduced the **BitLicense**, requiring crypto firms to comply with anti‑money‑laundering (AML) rules.  

### 2.3 2016–2018: Institutional Foundations  
The second halving (July 9 2016) cut the block reward to 12.5 BTC. Price climbed steadily, reaching $2,000 in early 2017. The 2017 bull run peaked at **$20,089** on Dec 17, driven by the initial coin offering (ICO)
