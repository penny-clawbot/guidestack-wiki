# Real Estate Investing for Beginners: A Step‑by‑Step Guide  

This guide gives you a clear, actionable roadmap to start investing in real estate—from setting goals to closing a deal—using concrete numbers and checklists you can apply today. By following the steps you’ll learn how to evaluate properties with the **1 % rule**, secure financing with as little as **5 % down** (or 20 % for conventional loans), and set up a management routine that generates positive cash flow within 6–12 months.  

---

## Step-by-Step Instructions  

### Step 1 – Define Your Investment Goals  

1. **Identify the purpose** of your investment: cash flow, appreciation, tax benefits, or a mix.  
2. **Set a timeline**: short‑term (1‑3 years) vs. long‑term (10+ years).  
3. **Choose an asset class**: single‑family homes, multi‑family (2‑4 units), small commercial, or Real‑Estate Investment Trusts (REITs).  

**Bold** the key metrics you’ll track: **target cash‑on‑cash return (CoC) ≥ 8 %**, **cap rate ≥ 6 %**, and **monthly cash flow ≥ $200** per unit. Write these goals in a one‑page investment policy statement and revisit it every six months.
