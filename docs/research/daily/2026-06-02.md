## Top 3 Findings

### 1. Google I/O 2026 — Search Rebuilt, Links Now an Afterthought
Google unveiled the biggest overhaul to Search since its inception. The new "intelligent search box" powered by Gemini 3.5 Flash ships this summer. Key changes:
- AI Mode (1B MAU) and AI Overviews (2.5B MAU) are now the default UI — users get AI-generated responses first, blue links second
- "Information agents" can track, synthesize, and alert on web changes autonomously (24/7)
- "Generative UI" builds custom widgets on-the-fly per query; traditional ranked links are pushed 1,674px below fold on AIO-triggered SERPs
- **Impact:** Cited sources get ~35% CTR lift over non-cited competitors; non-cited pages get nothing despite high domain authority

### 2. AI Overview Citations Shifted 50% Away from Top-10 Organic (Mar 2026)
Ahrefs' 863K-keyword study confirms top-10 organic results now account for only 38% of AI Overview citations, down from 76% in July 2025. The breakdown:
- 62% of citations come from pages ranking OUTSIDE the top 10 (via AI Mode fan-out sub-queries)
- Listicles account for 63% of LLM citations across 400M citations
- First 30% of a page captures 44.2% of all citations
- Google's official guidance (May 15, 2026) says "no need to chunk content" — but empirical data says passage-level specificity beats page-level breadth every time

### 3. llms.txt Officially Irrelevant for AI Visibility (Google, May 2026)
Google's Search Central published its first generative AI optimization guide (May 15, 2026): **llms.txt has no special treatment** for AI search visibility. The groundingpage.com (updated May 28, 2026) consolidates this: llms.txt remains useful as an agentic operating manual for inference-time navigation, but is NOT a GEO signal. This kills the "add llms.txt for AI SEO" tactic.

## Action Items for PSEO Network Template

1. **Rethink link-building strategy** — AI Mode citations don't follow PageRank; they follow relevance to sub-queries. Focus on topic-specific authority over domain-level authority.
2. **Prioritize listicle and FAQ content formats** — listicles = 63% of LLM citations. FAQ schema heavily favored for AIO citations.
3. **Optimize first 30% of every page** — front-load key facts, answers, and unique data. No more burying the lede.
4. **Update robots.txt template** — already correctly allows all major AI crawlers. Current configuration is sound.
