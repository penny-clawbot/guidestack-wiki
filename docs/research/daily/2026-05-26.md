Google Search team says llms.txt is NOT needed for AI Overviews/AI Mode. But Google's Lighthouse now checks for llms.txt as part of "agentic browsing readiness." These are two different Google product teams with opposing guidance. Adoption is rising but no major AI engine has formally endorsed the spec. Sites implementing it should not expect ranking benefits from Google directly — but it's becoming a dev-tool signal.

**Source:** [Search Engine Journal – Google's llms.txt Guidance Depends On Which Product You Ask](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/googles-llms-txt-guidance-depends-on-which-product-you-ask/575431/), May 20, 2026

## Action Items for PSEO Network Template

1. **Natural language query mapping** — Template pages should include FAQ/intent blocks that mirror how users will now phrase queries in the new conversational Search box. Update content blocks to handle long-form, descriptive prompts rather than short keywords.

2. **AI citation optimization** — Audit any template content that was built to "rank in AI Overviews" via structured manipulation. The May core update appears to be penalizing over-optimized AI citation tactics. Ensure E-E-A-T signals are genuine, not manufactured.

3. **Structured data alignment** — Schema markup remains critical for AI citation. Ensure the template's JSON-LD includes Article, FAQPage, and HowTo types where applicable, as AI systems rely heavily on structured data for source verification.

4. **llms.txt implementation** — Add llms.txt generation to the template's build process. Not for Google ranking benefit, but as an agentic-standard signal for future crawler compatibility. Keep expectations clear: it's a dev/best-practice move, not a ranking factor.
