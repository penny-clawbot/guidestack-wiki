# GEO/SEO Research Brief - June 5, 2026

## Top 3 Findings

1. **GEO Becomes Essential**: Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is no longer optional in 2026 as AI-powered search engines become mainstream. The practice of optimizing content to appear in AI-generated responses has evolved from traditional SEO focus on blue links to earning citations and visibility within LLM responses like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Mistral.

2. **Google's Agentic Shift**: Google I/O 2026 unveiled Gemini 3.5 Flash as the new default model for AI Mode, marking a significant shift toward agentic search capabilities. This update brings more sophisticated AI agents that can build and interact with websites through enhanced capabilities, transforming the browser into a proactive assistant.

3. **AI Crawler Segmentation**: Major AI vendors have split their crawlers - OpenAI runs separate bots for training (GPTBot) and search (OAI-SearchBot), while Anthropic operates three distinct bots. This segmentation requires new robots.txt strategies and creates different citation pathways for AI training vs. search visibility.

## Action Items for PSEO Network Template

1. **Update GEO Implementation**: Modify templates to include content structuring specifically for AI citation patterns, focusing on authoritative sources that get synthesized into responses.

2. **Implement AI Crawler Controls**: Add new robots.txt directives targeting both training and search bots separately, creating specific pathways for AI-generated content while protecting proprietary data.

3. **Enhance Structured Data**: Retire FAQ schema implementations and replace with new citation signals that align with Google's AI Mode optimization guidelines.

## Breaking Changes Needing Immediate Attention

1. **FAQ Schema Retirement**: Google is retiring FAQ rich results on May 7, 2026, requiring immediate schema reimplementation across all content pages.

2. **Programmatic SEO Crackdown**: Google's March 2026 update decimated thin templated content, requiring a shift to data-driven, high-quality implementations that include unique insights and E-E-A-T factors.

3. **Citation Tracking Systems**: Need to implement systems to track when and how our content gets cited by AI models, as this will become a primary visibility signal.
