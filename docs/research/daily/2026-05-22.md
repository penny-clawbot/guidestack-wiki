## 📊 Top 3 Findings

1. **GEO is real, llms.txt is not.** Multiple sources confirm llms.txt sees almost zero adoption by AI providers — it's been called "a dud." GEO strategy is now driven by structured data, authoritative citations, and semantic markup. The $7.3B GEO market (34% CAGR) is being won through schema + content quality, not text files at the root. *(Source: Medium/Medium.com, Feb 23; Stackmatix, Mar 21)*

2. **AI crawler landscape is stabilizing but diversifying.** OpenAI (GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot), Anthropic (ClaudeBot, Claude-User, Claude-SearchBot), Perplexity (PerplexityBot), and Google (Google-Extended) are all operating. All respect robots.txt. The crawler fleet is expanding but the "control" story via robots.txt is well-established. *(Source: nohacks.co, Apr 13; evolveamz.com, May 11)*

3. **No major schema.org changes this period.** Google's March 2026 core update touched structured data *eligibility*, but no significant new schema types or structured data formats have landed. The focus is on ensuring existing markup (Article, FAQPage, HowTo, Product, LocalBusiness) is comprehensive and AI-readable. *(Source: digitalapplied.com, Mar 20)*
