# Best Budget Hostels in Europe: A Step‑by‑Step Guide

This guide shows you exactly how to find, compare, and book the best budget hostels in Europe, with actionable steps, current price data (as of early 2024), and safety checklists. Follow the seven steps to secure high‑rated, affordable accommodation that fits your travel style and wallet.  

## Step‑by‑Step Instructions  


![Hero image for best budget hostels in europe](https://picsum.photos/seed/best-budget-hostels-in-europe-hero/1200/630)


### 1. Set a Realistic Daily Budget and Identify Target Cities  
- **Determine your all‑in budget:** Aim for €35–€50 per night if you want to keep total daily costs (food, transport, activities) under €100 in most Western‑European cities.  
- **Know regional price ranges:**  
  - **Western Europe (e.g., Paris, Amsterdam, London):** dorm beds average **€25–€35** per night.  
  - **Central Europe (e.g., Prague, Budapest, Krakow):** dorm beds average **€12–€20** per night.  
  - **Southern Europe (e.g., Barcelona, Lisbon, Athens):** dorm beds average **€18–€28** per night.  
- **Pick 2–3 cities** you want to visit and check the latest average hostel rates on **Hostelworld’s 2023 Year‑in‑Review** (they listed **over 18,000 hostels** across Europe).  

### 2. Use Trusted Hostel Aggregators with Advanced Filters  
- **Primary platforms:** **Hostelworld**, **Booking.com**, **Hostelz**, **Gomio**, and **Hostelling International (HI)**.  
- **Key filters to apply:**  
  - **Price range:** Set a max of €35 for dorms (adjust per region).  
  - **Rating:** ≥ 4.0 on Hostelworld or ≥ 8.0 on Booking.com.  
  - **Free Wi‑Fi, lockers, 24‑hour reception, breakfast** (saves €5–€10 daily).  
  - **Cancellation policy:** Choose “Free Cancellation” to keep flexibility.  
- **Pro tip:** Use the **“Map View”** on Hostelworld to verify the hostel’s distance from city centers and public transport hubs.  

### 3. Verify Ratings, Recent Reviews, and Safety Features  
- **Check the overall rating** (score out of 10) and the **number of reviews** (≥ 200 reviews indicates reliability).  
- **Read the 3–5 most recent reviews** (within the last 6 months) for up‑to‑date cleanliness, staff attitude, and noise levels.  
- **Safety checklist:**  
  - **24‑hour reception or secure entry** (key‑card or code).  
  - **Individual lockers** (bring your own padlock).  
  - **CCTV in common areas**.  
  - **Fire safety** (sprinklers, extinguishers, clear evacuation routes).  
- **Example:** *The Flying Pig Downtown* (Amsterdam) scores **9.2/10** on Hostelworld with 4,300+ reviews, offering key‑card entry, lockers, and a 24‑hour bar—perfect for solo travelers.
