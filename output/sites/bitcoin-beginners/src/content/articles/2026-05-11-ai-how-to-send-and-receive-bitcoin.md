# How to Send and Receive Bitcoin

To send Bitcoin you create a transaction from your wallet by entering the recipient’s address, specifying the amount, and confirming with your private key. To receive Bitcoin you provide your public Bitcoin address or QR code, allowing the sender to broadcast the transaction to the network.

## Sending Bitcoin: Step‑by‑Step

1. **Choose a wallet** – Use a software wallet (e.g., Exodus, BlueWallet) or a hardware wallet (e.g., Trezor, Ledger).  
2. **Fund the wallet** – Transfer Bitcoin from an exchange or another wallet.  
3. **Obtain the recipient’s address** – Copy the address (e.g., `bc1qxy2kgdygjrsqtzq2n0yrf2493p83kkfjhx0wlh`) or scan the QR code.  
4. **Enter the amount** – Specify how much BTC to send (e.g., 0.01 BTC).  
5. **Set the transaction fee** – Most wallets auto‑calculate a fee; you can adjust it. In early 2024 the median fee was **$1.46** (source: blockchain.com, Jan 2024). A typical fee for a standard transaction is **10 satoshis per virtual byte (sat/vB)**, which equals roughly **0.0002 BTC (≈ $5)** at a price of $25 000.  
6. **Confirm the transaction** – Review the details, enter your PIN or password, and broadcast.  
7. **Wait for confirmation** – The first block is usually mined in **≈ 10 minutes**; most exchanges require **1–6 confirmations** before crediting the deposit.

**Example:** Sending 0.01 BTC from a Coinbase account to a Trezor hardware wallet will cost about 0.0002 BTC in network fees and appear in the wallet after the first block is confirmed.

## Receiving Bitcoin: Best Practices

- **Generate a fresh address** for each transaction to improve privacy. Most modern wallets do this automatically (BIP‑47).  
- **Verify the address** – Check the first and last few characters of the address on the device screen, especially when using a hardware wallet.  
- **Use QR codes** for quick scanning
