## Frequently Asked Questions  

### What criteria should I use to evaluate a long‑term crypto hold?  
Focus on **fundamental health** (network activity, developer count, adoption), **tokenomics** (low inflation, clear issuance), **security** (audits, validator decentralization), and **market positioning** (market cap rank, competitive moat). Combine these with **technical trend** (price above 200‑week MA) and **sentiment** (social score > 70) for a balanced view.

### How often should I rebalance my portfolio?  
Rebalance **every six months** or when an asset deviates **> 5 %** from its target allocation. Frequent rebalancing can incur unnecessary transaction fees; a quarterly review is sufficient for most long‑term strategies.

### Is it safe to keep my holdings on an exchange?  
Exchanges are convenient but expose you to **custodial risk** (hacks, insolvency). Use exchanges only for **active trading** and move long‑term holdings to **hardware wallets** or **institutional‑grade custodians** (e.g., Coinbase Custody) for security.

### What are the tax implications of long‑term crypto holding in 2026?  
In most jurisdictions (U.S., EU, Canada), **capital gains tax** applies when you sell or exchange a crypto asset. Holding for **> 1 year** often qualifies for **long‑term capital gains**, which are taxed at a lower rate than short‑term gains. Keep detailed transaction records and consult a tax professional for jurisdiction‑specific.
