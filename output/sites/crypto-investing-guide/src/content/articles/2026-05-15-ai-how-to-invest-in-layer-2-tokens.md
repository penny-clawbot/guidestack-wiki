## Why Layer‑2 Tokens Matter

- **Scalability**: L2s process transactions off‑chain, reducing Ethereum’s mainnet congestion and lowering gas fees by **≈95 %** compared to L1.  
- **Ecosystem growth**: TVL on L2 networks grew from **$3 B** (Jan 2023) to **$25 B** (Mar 2026), a **733 %** increase (DeFiLlama).  
- **Yield opportunities**: Staking or providing liquidity on L2 protocols yields **4‑7 %** APR, often higher than L1 staking (Ethereum 2.0 ≈ 4.5 %).

## How to Buy & Store L2 Tokens


![Illustration for how to invest in layer 2 tokens](https://picsum.photos/seed/how-to-invest-in-layer-2-tokens-mid/1200/630)


1. **Centralized exchanges (CEX)**: Binance, Coinbase, Kraken list ARB, OP, MATIC, and ZKR.  
2. **Decentralized exchanges (DEX)**: Uniswap v3, SushiSwap, and Curve operate on Arbitrum, Optimism, and Polygon, offering direct wallet‑to‑wallet swaps.  
3. **Wallets**: Use non‑custodial wallets (MetaMask, Rabby, Argent) with **network switching** enabled to interact with each L2.  
4. **Hardware security**: Transfer large holdings to a hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor) and enable **Multi‑Signature (multisig)** protection for DAO‑governed tokens (OP, ZKR).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are layer‑2 tokens and why invest in them?

Layer‑2 tokens are the native assets of scaling solutions built on top of a base blockchain (most commonly Ethereum). They enable faster, cheaper transactions while still inheriting the security of the underlying L1. Investing in them gives exposure to the rapidly expanding DeFi, NFT, and GameFi ecosystems that rely on L2s for user experience.

### Which L2 token currently has the highest TVL?

As of March 2026, **Polygon (MATIC)** holds the highest absolute TVL at **$10.2 B**, followed closely by **Arbitrum** at **$7.4 B**. If you prioritize ecosystem size, MATIC offers the most capital‑locked, while ARB leads among pure‑EVM rollups.
