# Best Travel Rewards Credit Cards for Beginners

Travel rewards credit cards can seem intimidating when you're just starting your financial journey. Between annual fees, point valuations, blackout dates, and fine print that rivals a legal contract, it's no wonder many beginners give up before they ever earn their first free flight. But here's the thing: the best travel rewards cards for beginners are actually straightforward, affordable, and incredibly rewarding—if you know what to look for.

This guide cuts through the marketing noise and breaks down five travel rewards cards that genuinely work well for people who are new to the rewards game. We'll compare sign-up bonuses, earning rates, redemption options, and annual fees so you can make an informed decision without needing a finance degree.

---

## What Makes a Travel Card Beginner-Friendly?

Before we dive into specific cards, let's establish what separates a beginner-friendly travel card from one that belongs in a veteran's wallet.

**Key criteria include:**

- **Low or no annual fee** — You shouldn't pay big yearly fees just to access your own rewards
- **Straightforward earning structure** — Bonus categories should be simple, not requiring you to track rotating merchants
- **Flexible redemption options** — Points that transfer to multiple partners or can be used directly for travel bookings
- **Generous sign-up bonus** — A substantial initial return that jumpstarts your rewards balance
- **No foreign transaction fees** — Essential if you plan to use the card internationally

With these criteria in mind, let's examine the top contenders for beginners in 2024.

## Chase Sapphire Preferred: The Gold Standard for Beginners

The [Chase Sapphire Preferred](https://www.chase.com/) sits at the top of most beginner-friendly travel card lists, and for good reason. It offers an exceptional balance of value, simplicity, and flexibility that few cards can match.

**Sign-Up Bonus:** Earn 60,000 bonus points after spending $4,000 on purchases in the first 3 months from account opening. That's worth approximately $750 in travel booked through Chase Travel℠.

**Earning Rates:**

- 5x points on travel purchased through Chase Travel℠
- 3x points on dining (including eligible delivery services)
- 3x points on select streaming services
- 2x points on all other travel purchases
- 1x points on everything else

**Annual Fee:** $95

**Key Benefits:**

- Points transfer to 14 airline and hotel partners at a 25% bonus when redeemed through Chase Travel℠
- No foreign transaction fees
- Trip cancellation/interruption insurance
- Primary rental car insurance coverage
- Baggage delay insurance up to $100 per day for 5 days

The Sapphire Preferred's strength lies in its versatility. Whether you want to book directly with airlines, transfer points to partners like United, Southwest, or Hyatt, or use your points for statement credits, this card handles it all without punishing complexity.

## Capital One VentureOne Rewards: No-Nonsense Flat Rate Earning

If simplicity is your priority, the [Capital One VentureOne Rewards](https://www.capitalone.com/) card strips away the category bonuses and gives you a straightforward 1.25x miles on every purchase, everywhere.

**Sign-Up Bonus:** Earn 20,000 bonus miles when you spend $500 on purchases within the first 3 months from account opening. Miles are worth 1 cent each, so that's a $200 statement credit toward any travel purchase.

**Earning Rates:**

- 5x miles on hotels and rental cars booked through Capital One Travel
- 1.25x miles on all purchases

**Annual Fee:** $0 (no annual fee)

**Key Benefits:**

- No annual fee whatsoever
- No foreign transaction fees
- Up to $120 credit for Global Entry or TSA PreCheck application
- Travel protections including auto rental coverage and travel accident insurance
- Miles don't expire as long as your account is open

The VentureOne card is perfect for beginners who don't want to track which category earns what bonus. Every dollar you spend earns the same rate, and you can redeem miles as statement credits against any travel expense—no minimum redemption, no blackout dates.

## Discover it Miles: Cash Back Meets Travel Rewards

The [Discover it Miles](https://www.discover.com/credit-cards/it/miles.html) card takes an interesting approach by functioning as a cash back card that can be used like a travel rewards card. Discover matches all the miles you earn in your first year, making it one of the most generous offers available.

**Sign-Up Bonus:** Discover matches ALL miles earned at the end of your first year—there's no spending threshold to unlock the match.

**Earning Rates:**

- Unlimited 1.5x miles on every purchase
- All miles matched by Discover at year-end (effectively 3x miles in year one)

**Annual Fee:** $0

**Key Benefits:**

- No annual fee
- No foreign transaction fees
- Miles can be redeemed as statement credits for travel purchases or cash
- No blackout dates or restrictions
- Free FICO score monitoring
- Social Security number monitored for identity theft alerts

The Discover it Miles shines with its first-year match promotion. If you spend $15,000 in your first year (the national average for credit card spending), you'd earn 22,500 miles, which Discover would double to 45,000 miles—worth $450 in travel redemptions.

**Earning Rates:**

- 1.5x points on every purchase with no limits

**Annual Fee:** $0

**Key Benefits:**

- No annual fee
- No foreign transaction fees

- No blackout dates or airline restrictions
- Access to Bank of America Travel Center for additional booking discounts
- Complimentary access to ShopSafe online shopping protection

The Bank of America Travel Rewards card works exceptionally well for beginners who bank with Bank of America, as Preferred Rewards members can earn up to a 75% bonus on all earning rates—potentially pushing the base rate to 2.625x points on every purchase.

**Travel protections:** As a new traveler, you'll appreciate that the Sapphire Preferred includes primary rental car coverage, trip cancellation insurance, and baggage delay protection. These benefits alone can save hundreds of dollars during a single trip.

**Scalability:** When you're ready to level up your rewards strategy, the Sapphire Preferred pairs seamlessly with Chase Freedom cards that offer 5% rotating bonus categories, effectively multiplying your earning potential without adding annual fees.

The Sapphire Preferred isn't just the best beginner card—it's a card you'll likely keep for years even after graduating
