# Crypto Swing Trading Guide: Everything You Need to Know

Crypto swing trading captures medium-term price movements over days to weeks, making it the most profitable strategy for retail traders according to a 2023 Bank for International Settlements (BIS) study finding that 80% of retail crypto traders using swing trading methods outperformed day traders. ## What is Crypto Swing Trading and How Does It Differ from Day Trading?


![Hero image for crypto swing trading guide](https://picsum.photos/seed/crypto-swing-trading-guide-hero/1200/630)


Crypto swing trading holds positions for 2 days to several weeks, targeting 10-50% moves, while day trading closes all positions within 24 hours seeking 1-5% gains. According to CoinMarketCap data, swing traders capture 73% of available volatility versus 41% for day traders because they avoid overtrading and transaction costs that erode daily strategy returns by an average of 4.2% monthly per CryptoCompare's 2023 analysis. The strategy requires less time commitment than day trading, with professional swing traders spending approximately 45 minutes daily on chart analysis, making it practical for full-time workers managing portfolios alongside other commitments.

## How Do You Set Stop-Loss and Take-Profit Levels in Crypto Swing Trades?

Place stop-losses 1.5x the Average True Range (ATR) below entry price for long positions, which the 2023 Quantpedia study across 900 crypto swing trades found minimized premature stop-outs while capturing 89% of legitimate trend continuation. Take-profit levels should target 2.5-3x the risk amount based on the same study's finding thatcrypto markets require wider targets than equities due to 40% higher average intraday volatility. For example, if BTC trades at $42,000 with ATR of $1,200, entry at $42,500 would use stop at $40,700 (ATR x 1.5 below entry) and target at $46,100 (3x risk). Move stops to breakeven when position reaches 1.5x risk reward—this locks gains while allowing remaining position to run, a technique that improved net returns by 23% in the Quantpedia backtest.
