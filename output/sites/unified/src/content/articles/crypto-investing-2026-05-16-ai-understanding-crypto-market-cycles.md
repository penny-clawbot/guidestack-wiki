# Understanding Crypto Market Cycles  

Crypto market cycles are repeating patterns of price appreciation and decline that follow a recognizable sequence of phases: accumulation, markup, distribution, and markdown. Historically, each Bitcoin cycle has lasted roughly 2–4 years, with peak‑to‑trough declines averaging 70–80 % (e.g., 2017–2018, 2021–2022). Recognizing these phases helps investors time entries, manage risk, and avoid common behavioral pitfalls.

## The Four Phases of a Crypto Market Cycle  


![Hero image for understanding crypto market cycles](https://picsum.photos/seed/understanding-crypto-market-cycles-hero/1200/630)


- **Accumulation** – After a market bottom, price is low, volume subdued, and sentiment is “Extreme Fear.” Institutional players and early adopters begin to buy quietly.  
- **Markup** – Price starts rising, media coverage expands, and retail FOMO (fear of missing out) kicks in. Volume climbs and new narratives (DeFi, NFTs, Layer‑2s) emerge.  
- **Distribution** – Smart money begins selling, price plateaus, and on‑chain metrics show large holders moving assets to exchanges. Fear & Greed Index hovers near “Greed.”  
- **Markdown** – Price falls sharply, often 70–80 % from the peak, as market sentiment flips to “Fear” or “Extreme Fear,” completing the cycle.

These phases are observable across Bitcoin, Ethereum, and most altcoins, though amplitude and duration vary.

During the **markup phase** of the 2020‑2021 cycle, Bitcoin surged from ~$10,000 in March 2020 to $64,800 in April 2021 – a **548 % gain** in just 13 months. Ethereum rose from $250 to $4,300 (1,620 % gain) during the same window, illustrating how altcoins often amplify Bitcoin’s moves.

## Key Indicators for Identifying Cycle Phases  


![Illustration for understanding crypto market cycles](https://picsum.photos/seed/understanding-crypto-market-cycles-mid/1200/630)


- **MVRV Ratio (Market Value to Realized Value)** – Values below 0.5 signal accumulation; above 3.5 suggest distribution. According to Glassnode (2023), the MVRV dropped to 0.49 in July 2022, marking a historic accumulation window.  
- **Fear & Greed Index** – Continuously “Extreme Fear” (<20) aligns with markdown or early accumulation; readings above 70 indicate greed typical of distribution.  
- **Exchange Net Flows** – High inflows to exchanges often precede distribution, while outflows indicate hodlers accumulating. CryptoQuant reported a net outflow of 120,000.
