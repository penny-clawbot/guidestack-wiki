# How to Start Yield Farming: A Complete Step-by-Step Guide

Yield farming is a DeFi strategy where you earn passive income by locking cryptocurrency assets into liquidity pools or lending platforms. This guide walks you through everything you need to start yield farming in 2025, from setting up a wallet to maximizing returns while managing risk.

## Step-by-Step Instructions


![Hero image for how to start yield farming](https://picsum.photos/seed/how-to-start-yield-farming-hero/1200/630)


### Step 1: Set Up a Cryptocurrency Wallet

The first requirement for yield farming is a Web3-compatible wallet. **MetaMask** remains the most popular option, with over 30 million monthly active users as of January 2025. Download the browser extension or mobile app from metamask.io and create a secure wallet. Write down your 12-word seed phrase on paper and store it in a secure location—never digitize it.

For advanced users, **Rabby** or **Frame** wallets offer lower gas fees on Ethereum transactions. If you plan to farm on multiple chains, consider a **multi-chain wallet** like Coin98 or(TokenPocket to manage assets across networks without switching between multiple wallets.

### Step 2: Acquire Starting Capital

Deposit initial capital into your wallet. You need at least **$500–$1,000** to make yield farming profitable after accounting for gas fees, especially on Ethereum mainnet where transaction costs frequently exceed $20–$50 per swap (as of Q1 2025). Popular starting assets include:

- **ETH** (for Ethereum-based farms)
- **USDC/USDT** (stablecoins for low-risk lending)
- **WBTC** (for BTC-collateralized strategies)

Purchase crypto through exchanges like Coinbase, Binance, or Kraken, then transfer to your wallet. Always double-check the receiving address—transactions on blockchains are irreversible.

### Step 3: Connect to a DeFi Protocol

Choose a decentralized exchange or lending platform. As of February 2025, the highest-volume protocols include:

- **Uniswap** (largest DEX by volume, $1.2T+ all-time)
- **Aave** (leading lending protocol with $20B+ TVL)
- **Curve Finance** (dominant for stablecoin pools)
- **GMX** (perpetual futures with 60%+ APR potential)

Visit the protocol's website and click "Connect Wallet." MetaMask will prompt you to approve the connection. Always verify you're on the correct URL—phishing sites are common. Bookmark official protocol pages to avoid scams.

### Step 4: Select Your Yield Farming Strategy

Your risk tolerance determines your strategy:

| Strategy | Risk Level | Typical APR | Best For |
|----------|------------|-------------|----------|
| Stablecoin Lending | Low | 3–12% | Conservative investors |
| Liquidity Provision (Stable pairs) | Low-Medium | 5–25% | Steady income seekers |
| Liquidity Provision (Volatile pairs) | High | 20–200%+ | Aggressive growth |
| Leveraged Yield Farming | Very High | 50–500%+ | Experienced traders |

For beginners, **stablecoin lending on Aave or Compound** offers the safest entry point. As of February 2025, USDC on Aave earns approximately **4.5–6% APY**, while DAI earns **4.2–5.8%**.

### Step 5: Approve Tokens and Deposit Funds

Once you've selected a pool or lending market:

1. **Approve token spending** — Click "Approve" in the protocol interface; this costs gas but only needs to be done once per token.
2. **Deposit your assets** — Click "Supply" or "Deposit" and enter the amount. MetaMask will show the gas fee estimate.
3. **Confirm the transaction** — Review the gas fee; if it's higher than 2% of your deposit, wait for lower network congestion (typically late night US hours).

On Ethereum, average gas fees in early 2025 range from **$3–$15** for simple transactions. For cheaper options, consider farming on **Arbitrum** (average fees under $0.50) or **Base** (fees under $0.10).

### Step 6: Monitor Positions and Reinvest Returns

After depositing, your assets begin earning yield—often distributed in the protocol's native token or the same asset you deposited. Check your dashboard regularly to monitor:

- **APY changes** — Yields fluctuate daily based on pool utilization and market conditions
- **Impermanent loss** — For volatile asset pairs, price divergence can erode returns
- **Gas optimization** — Compound rewards only when gas fees are low

Many protocols display "Net APR" which accounts for token incentives. As of 2025, a pool showing 25% APR may deliver only 12–15% net after impermanent loss and token inflation.

### Step 7: Withdraw and Exit Safely

When you're ready to exit:

1. Go to the protocol's dashboard and locate your active position
2. Click **"Withdraw"** or **"Remove Liquidity"**
3. Confirm the transaction in your wallet
4. Wait for block confirmation (usually under 2 minutes on L2 networks)

After withdrawing, you can swap earned tokens for stablecoins to lock in profits or reinvest in higher-yielding opportunities.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the minimum amount needed to start yield farming?

The minimum viable amount depends on your target chain. For Ethereum mainnet, **$1,000+** is recommended because gas fees often exceed $20–$50 per transaction, eating into smaller deposits. On Layer 2 networks like Arbitrum, Base, or Optimism, you can start with **$100–$200** as transaction fees typically stay below $1.

### Is yield farming safe?

Yield farming carries multiple risks including smart contract exploits, impermanent loss, rug pulls, and regulatory uncertainty. In 2026 alone, approximately **$400 million** was lost to DeFi exploits. Use protocols with audited smart contracts (check audits on OpenZeppelin or Trail of Bits), never invest more than you can afford to lose, and start with established protocols like Aave, Compound, or Uniswap.

### How often should I check my yield farming positions?

Check positions at minimum **once daily** during your first month to understand how yields fluctuate. Automated tools like **Zerion**, **DeBank**, or **Zapper** aggregate your positions across multiple protocols. Set price alerts for assets you've supplied to catch sudden depegs or protocol issues. Weekly rebalancing is sufficient once you're comfortable with the strategy.

### What taxes apply to yield farming income?

Yield farming rewards are generally treated as **ordinary income** in most jurisdictions, taxed in the year received. In the United States, per IRS guidance, crypto-to-crypto swaps can trigger capital gains events. As of 2025, the EU's MiCA regulation and varying state-level US rules create complex compliance requirements. Consult a crypto-specialized tax professional and maintain detailed records of all transactions, timestamps, and USD values.

## Tips


![Illustration for how to start yield farming](https://picsum.photos/seed/how-to-start-yield-farming-mid/1200/630)


- **Start with testnet funds** — Use Ethereum Sepolia or Arbitrum Sepolia testnets to practice transactions before committing real capital.
- **Diversify across protocols** — Never deposit more than 20% of your capital into a single protocol to reduce smart contract risk.
- **Track gas costs** — Use tools like **Etherscan Gas Tracker** or **Gas Dao** to find optimal transaction times. Average savings of 40–60% are possible by farming during off-peak hours.
- **Watch for incentive programs** — Protocols frequently launch liquidity mining programs offering 2–5x regular APY. As of early 2025, protocols like Pendle, EigenLayer, and Berachain have launched lucrative incentivized pools.
- **Calculate true net yield** — Subtract impermanent loss, token inflation, and gas costs from advertised APR to determine actual returns.
- **Use hardware wallets for large positions** — Ledger and Trezor devices provide additional security layers for portfolios exceeding $10,000.
