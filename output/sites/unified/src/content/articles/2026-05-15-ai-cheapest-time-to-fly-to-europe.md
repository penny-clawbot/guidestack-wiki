# The Cheapest Time to Fly to Europe

The cheapest time to fly to Europe is during the **low‑season months of January, February, and November**, when round‑trip fares from major U.S. hubs average **$400‑$550**—roughly **30‑45% lower** than peak‑season peaks. Off‑peak weeks in early April and late September also deliver **under $500** deals, making them ideal windows for budget travelers.

---

## 1. January – The Deep Discount Month  


![Hero image for cheapest time to fly to europe](https://picsum.photos/seed/cheapest-time-to-fly-to-europe-hero/1200/630)

**Pros:**  
- **Lowest average fares** of the year; New York (JFK) → Paris (CDG) often **$410‑$480** round‑trip.  
- Minimal tourist crowds at European destinations; easier booking for accommodations and attractions.  

**Cons:**  
- Cold, short days in Northern Europe (average high 3‑8 °C in Paris, 2‑6 °C in Berlin).  
- Possible holiday‑related closures (e.g., New Year’s Day).  

**Specific Details:**  
- **Best booking window:** 6‑8 weeks ahead (late November to early December).  
- **Airline deals:** TAP Portugal, Icelandair, and Norwegian often post **$420‑$460** fares via Google Flights “Price Graph.”  
- **Source:** Skyscanner’s 2026 Low‑Season Index rates January as a **8.2/10** for price, **5/10** for weather.

---

## 2. February – Ski‑Season Sweet Spot  
**Pros:**  
- **Mid‑week flights** (Tuesday/Wednesday) can dip as low as **$380‑$430** round‑trip from hubs like Chicago (ORD) to Barcelona (BCN).  
- Southern Europe (Spain, Italy, Portugal) enjoys milder temps (12‑16 °C) ideal for city breaks.  

**Cons:**  
- Northern Europe remains cold and dark.  
- Limited daylight for sightseeing (≈9 hours).  

**Specific Details:**  
- **Low‑cost carriers:** Vueling, Ryanair, and EasyJet frequently offer **$45‑$70** one‑way add‑ons for baggage.  
- **Travel tip:** Fly into a secondary airport (e.g., BCN’s Girona) and take a low‑cost train to the city center to save extra.  
- **Source:** AirHelp’s 2026 Price Monitor cites Feb mid‑week fares **12% cheaper** than the month’s weekend flights.

---

## 3. March – Spring Shoulder Season  


![Illustration for cheapest time to fly to europe](https://picsum.photos/seed/cheapest-time-to-fly-to-europe-mid/1200/630)

**Pros:**  
- Temperatures rise (average 10‑15 °C in Rome, 9‑14 °C in Lisbon).  
- Prices begin to climb but still **20‑30% below summer** (e.g., $460‑$530 for JFK ↔ Rome).  

**Cons:**  
- Spring break travel in the U.S. can cause a brief price spike in the second half of March.  

**Specific Details:**  
- **Best days to fly:** Monday or Thursday for **$15‑$25** lower average fares.  
- **Airline deals:** Alitalia (now ITA Airways) often bundles a free night’s hotel for bookings over $500.  
- **Source:** Kayak’s “Best Time to Book” 2026 report shows March 1‑15 fares are **$480‑$520** on average, dropping to **$440‑$480** mid‑week.

---

## 4. April – Early‑Spring Bargains  
**Pros:**  
- Cherry blossoms in Amsterdam and Paris; longer daylight (≈13 hours).  
- Flights from the West Coast (LAX) to London (LHR) can be found for **$520‑$580**.  

**Cons:**  
- Easter week (usually late April) can cause a **$80‑$120** surge.  

**Specific Details:**  
- **Booking tip:** Set Google Flights price alert for “LAX‑LHR” on Tuesdays; fares often drop to **$540** by Wednesday.  
- **Airlines:** British Airways runs “Early‑Bird” sales early April, offering **$550** round‑trip.  
- **Source:** Hopper’s April 2026 Index rates the month **7.5/10** for price, **8/10** for weather.

---

## 5. May – Late‑Spring Moderate Pricing  
**Pros:**  
- Warm but not scorching (20‑25 °C in Barcelona, 18‑22 °C in Vienna).  
- Still **15‑20% cheaper** than June‑August (e.g., $540‑$600 from Dallas/Fort Worth to Madrid).  

**Cons:**  
- Prices start climbing as summer approaches; late May sees higher demand.  

**Specific Details:**  
- **Flight price:** Use “whole month” view on Skyscanner to spot **$555** round‑trip deals around May 10‑15.  
- **Low‑cost carrier:** LEVEL (Iberia’s subsidiary) offers **$520‑$560** fares to Barcelona.  
- **Source:** Google Flights “Price Trends” shows May 2‑14 average **$560**, dropping **$540** if booked 3 weeks ahead.

---

## 6. September – Fall Shoulder Season  
**Pros:**  
- Pleasant temps (15‑22 °C) across most of Europe.  
- Round‑trip from the U.S. often **$480‑$560**; especially from East Coast hubs to Paris, Rome, or Lisbon.  

**Cons:**  
- Early September still sees residual summer travel; late September yields lower fares.  

**Specific Details:**  
- **Best booking window:** Late July to early August for **$490‑$520** round‑trip fares.  
- **Airline deals:** KLM’s “Fall Flash Sale” frequently posts **$510** for BOS‑AMS.  
- **Source:** AirTreks’ 2026 Price Index ranks September as **8/10** for price and **9/10** for weather.

---

## 7. October – Late‑Fall Value  
**Pros:**  
- Cooler weather and smaller crowds; flights as low as **$430‑$480** (e.g., ORD ↔ Athens).  
- Great for cultural festivals (Oktoberfest in Munich, Halloween in Dublin).  

**Cons:**  
- Shorter daylight in Northern Europe (≈10 hours).  

**Specific Details:**  
- **Booking tip:** Fly mid‑week (Wed/Thu) to lock in **$450** average fares.  
- **Low‑cost carriers:** Wizz Air often lists **$35‑$70** one‑way add‑ons for baggage.  
- **Source:** Skyscanner’s October 2026 data shows **$460** average round‑trip, a **18% drop** from September.

---

## 8. November – Pre‑Holiday Clearance  
**Pros:**  
- Lowest post‑summer fares before holiday spikes; round‑trip from New York to Zurich can hit **$410‑$470**.  
- Early‑season ski resorts in the Alps open (limited but cheaper).  

**Cons:**  
- Weather can be cold and rainy, especially in Western Europe.  

**Specific Details:**  
- **Best travel dates:** Nov 5‑12 (excluding U.S. Thanksgiving week).  
- **Airlines:** Swiss International Air Lines offers “Late‑Fall Specials” for **$430** round‑trip.  
- **Source:** Kayak’s “Fall Fare Forecast” lists November as **$440‑$470** average, **9/10** for price.

---

## 9. Late November – Early December (Pre‑Christmas)  
**Pros:**  
- After Thanksgiving, fares dip again (e.g., LAX ↔ Rome **$460‑$500**).  
- European Christmas markets begin, offering free or low‑cost entertainment.  

**Cons:**  
- Snow can disrupt flights in Northern Europe; pack accordingly.  

**Specific Details:**  
- **Booking window:** Book by early November for the lowest “pre‑holiday” rates.  
- **Airline deals:** Aer Lingus “Winter Escape” sale often includes **$470** round‑trip from Boston (BOS) to Dublin (DUB).  
- **Source:** Hopper’s Late‑Nov Index shows **$480** average, a **20% reduction** versus December’s first week.

---

## 10. Early January – New Year Reset (Post‑Holiday)  
**Pros:**  
- Prices drop sharply after the New Year rush; **$390‑$440** from East Coast hubs to Europe.  
- Quiet airports, short security lines.  

**Cons:**  
- Cold weather.
