# Best Hardware Wallets Compared for 2026

The **Ledger Nano X** is the best all‑around choice for most investors because it combines a certified secure element, Bluetooth convenience, and support for more than 5,500 tokens at a reasonable $149 price. If you demand fully open‑source firmware and a vivid touchscreen, the **Trezor Model T** ($179) is the top pick for privacy‑focused users. For the highest level of air‑gap security and Bitcoin‑only features, the **Coldcard Mk4** ($149) remains the preferred device among power users.

### Key Takeaways from the Table

- **Security Certification:** Devices with a Common Criteria (CC) **EAL5+** or **EAL6+** rating provide the highest assurance that private keys never leave the secure chip. Ledger, Coldcard, ELLIPAL, BitBox02, and SecuX all meet this bar.
- **Air‑Gap Capability:** ELLIPAL Titan and Coldcard Mk4 offer true air‑gap operation via QR codes or SD cards, eliminating any wired attack surface.
- **Connectivity:** Bluetooth on the Nano X, Ledger Stax, and D’CENT enables convenient mobile use, while USB‑C is universal across newer models.
- **Battery Life:** The Nano X leads with up to 8 months of standby, followed by the SecuX V20 (≈60 days). Most other devices rely on USB power.
- **Supported Tokens:** SafePal S1 and ELLIPAL Titan claim support for **10,000+** assets, making them ideal for DeFi explorers. Ledger Nano X and SecuX V20 are close behind with **5,500+**.
- **Open‑Source Status:** Trezor Model T, Coldcard Mk4, BitBox02, and Trezor Safe 3 provide fully open firmware, appealing to users who want full code transparency.
