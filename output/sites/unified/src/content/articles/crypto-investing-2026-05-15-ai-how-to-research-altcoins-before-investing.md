# How to Research Altcoins Before Investing

To research altcoins effectively, you must evaluate the project's technology, tokenomics, team credibility, and market metrics before committing capital. Essential steps include reading the whitepaper, checking developer activity on GitHub, analyzing trading volume and liquidity, and verifying team backgrounds. Research indicates that approximately **90% of cryptocurrency projects eventually fail**, making thorough due diligence critical for preserving capital in the highly speculative altcoin market.

## Evaluate the Project's Fundamentals and Technology


![Hero image for how to research altcoins before investing](https://picsum.photos/seed/how-to-research-altcoins-before-investing-hero/1200/630)


The foundation of any altcoin investment thesis begins with understanding the problem the project solves and whether its technology delivers a genuine advantage over existing solutions. Start by reading the project's whitepaper—ideally one that is detailed, technically sound, and free of unrealistic promises. For example, **Ethereum (ETH)** distinguished itself from earlier cryptocurrencies by introducing smart contract functionality, solving the limitation of Bitcoin's purely transactional design.

When evaluating technology, examine the blockchain's architecture, consensus mechanism, and scalability solutions. Projects like **Solana (SOL)** gained attention by offering proof-of-stake combined with proof-of-history, achieving theoretical throughput of 65,000 transactions per second compared to Ethereum's ~30 transactions per second pre-upgrades. However, Solana experienced multiple network outages in 2021-2022, demonstrating that performance claims must be validated against real-world reliability.

Check the project's code repositories on GitHub to assess development activity. Active repositories with regular commits indicate ongoing development. A project with fewer than 100 total commits in two years may signal abandoned development. Additionally, verify whether the code has undergone third-party security audits from firms like **CertiK, Consensys Diligence, or Trail of Bits**—audit reports should be publicly available on the project's website or GitHub.

## Analyze Tokenomics and Economic Model

Tokenomics fundamentally determines whether an altcoin creates value or suffers from structural inflation that erodes holder wealth over time. Key metrics to examine include total supply, circulating supply, inflation rate, and token distribution.

**Total supply** refers to the maximum number of tokens that will ever exist, while **circulating supply** is the number currently available in the market. Tokens with massive total supplies (often in billions or trillions) may appear cheap per unit but can mask enormous fully-diluted valuations. For instance, **Shiba Inu (SHIB)** has a total supply of 1 quadrillion, making individual tokens nearly worthless despite a multi-billion dollar market cap.

Examine the token distribution model carefully. Ideally, no single entity should control more than **20-30% of total tokens**. High concentration among founders, venture capital investors, or team members creates dump risk, where large holders sell en masse, crashing the price. Projects that publish detailed allocation schedules, including vesting periods for team and investor tokens, demonstrate transparency.

Assess the token's utility within the ecosystem. Does it serve governance functions (as **Compound's COMP** does), provide fee discounts (like **Binance Coin BNB**), or enable staking rewards? Tokens with genuine utility have stronger demand drivers than those merely functioning as speculative vehicles. Additionally, examine the inflation schedule—tokens with annual inflation rates above **10-15%** may face persistent selling pressure from newly minted tokens entering circulation.

## Assess Team Credibility and Development Activity


![Illustration for how to research altcoins before investing](https://picsum.photos/seed/how-to-research-altcoins-before-investing-mid/1200/630)


The human element behind an altcoin project significantly influences its probability of long-term success. Research the founding team thoroughly by checking professional backgrounds, previous blockchain experience, and verifiable identities.

Projects with anonymous or pseudonymous teams, while common in crypto, carry elevated risk. **SushiSwap's** anonymous founder "Chef Nomi" famously sold $13 million worth of SUSHI tokens in 2020, crashing the price by 75%—an event that underscores the risks of trusting anonymous developers with your capital. In contrast, projects like **Polygon (MATIC)** benefited from identifiable founders with established credentials in cryptography and blockchain development.

Look for advisory board members with relevant industry experience. Projects backed by established venture capital firms such as **a16z, Polychain Capital, or Paradigm** have undergone due diligence by professional investors, though this does not guarantee success. Cross-reference claimed partnerships and integrations—some projects exaggerate or fabricate relationships with major companies.

Evaluate the project's communication channels and transparency practices. Active communities on Discord, Telegram, or Reddit with engaged developers responding to questions indicate healthy projects. Weekly or biweekly development updates on Medium or official blogs demonstrate accountability. Conversely, projects that go silent for months or exhibit defensive behavior when questioned often hide problems.

## Review Market Metrics and Community Health

Market data provides essential context for understanding price dynamics, liquidity, and social sentiment surrounding an altcoin. Trading volume and liquidity determine how easily you can buy or sell without significantly impacting the price.

A healthy altcoin should maintain **daily trading volume exceeding $1-5 million** for moderate-sized positions to be executed without substantial slippage. Low-volume tokens are vulnerable to price manipulation, where large holders ("whales") can trigger artificial price movements to trap retail traders.

Examine exchange listings. Altcoins traded on reputable exchanges like **Coinbase, Binance, or Kraken** have undergone some level of vetting. However, being listed on numerous obscure exchanges may indicate a marketing push rather than genuine adoption. CheckCoinGecko or CoinMarketCap for verified exchange listings and be wary of projects claiming listings they don't actually have.

Community health assessment includes social media following, sentiment analysis, and developer engagement. A project with 500,000 Twitter followers but only 100 active participants in its Discord may indicate inflated hype rather than genuine interest. Use tools like LunarCrush or Santiment to analyze social sentiment and track whether positive discussions correlate with actual development progress.

**On-chain metrics** reveal user adoption patterns: active addresses, transaction counts, and smart contract interactions provide objective data on network utilization. A token with rising prices but declining active addresses suggests unsustainable speculation rather than organic growth.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I identify altcoin scams before investing?

Red flags include anonymous teams with no verifiable track records, guaranteed returns or "too good to be true" promises, plagiarized whitepapers, sudden social media hype without substance, and tokens with extremely high supply concentrations among few addresses. Always verify information independently through multiple sources before committing funds.

### Should I invest based on a project's social media popularity?

Social media popularity alone is insufficient for investment decisions. While strong communities can drive initial interest, sustainable value comes from genuine utility, adoption, and development progress. Many pump-and-dump schemes artificially inflate social media presence to attract victims before creators sell their holdings.

### How much of my portfolio should I allocate to altcoins?

Financial experts typically recommend allocating **5-20% of your total cryptocurrency portfolio** to altcoins, with the remainder in established assets like Bitcoin and Ethereum. Altcoins carry substantially higher risk than dominant cryptocurrencies, making position sizing critical for portfolio survival if multiple investments fail.

## Conclusion

Researching altcoins requires systematic evaluation across technology, tokenomics, team credibility, and market metrics. No single factor guarantees success, but projects excelling across multiple dimensions generally offer better risk-adjusted opportunities. Always verify claims independently, start with small positions to validate your thesis, and remember that due diligence is an ongoing process as market conditions and project fundamentals evolve continuously.
