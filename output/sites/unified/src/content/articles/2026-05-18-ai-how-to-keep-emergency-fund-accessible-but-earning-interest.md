# How to Keep Your Emergency Fund Accessible but Earning Interest  

**Top picks:** The most effective way to keep your emergency fund both liquid and interest‑bearing is to park it in a high‑yield savings account (currently 4.5‑5.0 % APY), a money‑market account (≈4.3‑4.7 % APY), or a short‑term Treasury‑bill ladder (≈5.0‑5.3 % APY). These options combine FDIC or government insurance with competitive yields and same‑day or next‑day access.

---

## 1. High‑Yield Savings Account – Marcus by Goldman Sachs  


![Hero image for how to keep emergency fund accessible but earning interest](https://picsum.photos/seed/how-to-keep-emergency-fund-accessible-but-earning-interest-hero/1200/630)


**Pros**  
- **APY:** **4.65 %** (as of March 2026, no promo rate)  
- **FDIC‑insured** up to $250,000 per depositor  
- No monthly fees, $0 minimum opening deposit  
- Fast online transfers (same‑business‑day if request before 1 p.m. ET)  

**Cons**  
- Limited to six pre‑authorized transfers per statement cycle (federal regulation)  
- No debit card or check writing  

**Details**  
Marcus offers a straightforward savings vehicle with a **4.65 % APY**, ranking it among the top 5 online savings accounts per Bankrate’s March 2026 survey. The account is FDIC‑insured (Cert # 26770) and includes a user‑friendly mobile app with 24/7 customer support. Transfers to external banks are free and typically complete within one business day.  

*Source: Bankrate.com, “Best High‑Yield Savings Accounts,” March 2026; FDIC.gov.*

## 2. Money‑Market Account – Ally Bank  

**Pros**  
- **APY:** **4.30 %** on balances ≥ $0  
- **FDIC‑insured** up to $250,000  
- Debit card + limited check writing (up to $500 per day)  
- No monthly maintenance fees  

**Cons**  
- The debit card is a “Money Market Card,” not a traditional Visa/Mastercard  
- Higher withdrawal limits still cap at $1,000 per day for ATM withdrawals  

**Details**  
Ally’s Money Market Account currently yields **4.30 % APY** and provides a hybrid of savings and checking features. The account includes a free Maestro‑branded debit card for ATM access and limited check writing, making it suitable for emergencies that require cash. The account’s mobile app also supports instant transfers to external accounts.  

*Source: Ally.com, “Money Market Account Rates,” March 2026; FDIC Bank Find.*

## 3. Interest‑Bearing Checking – SoFi Checking & Savings  


![Illustration for how to keep emergency fund accessible but earning interest](https://picsum.photos/seed/how-to-keep-emergency-fund-accessible-but-earning-interest-mid/1200/630)


**Pros**  
- **APY:** **0.50 %** on Checking; **4.50 %** on Savings (when direct deposit ≥ $3,000/month)  
- **FDIC‑insured** via partner banks (up to $250,000)  
- No fees, early direct deposit up to 2 days early  
- Built‑in budgeting tools  

**Cons**  
- The high **4.50 %** APY on Savings requires meeting the direct‑deposit threshold  
- Limited physical branches (online‑only)  

**Details**  
SoFi’s Checking & Savings bundle offers a **0.50 % APY** on the checking component and a boosted **4.50 % APY** on the savings side when you set up a qualifying direct deposit. The account is FDIC‑insured through partner banks and includes early paycheck access, making it a flexible option for those who want a single‑platform solution.  

*Source: SoFi.com, “Checking & Savings Rates,” March 2026; FDIC Deposit Insurance Summary.*

**Cons**  
- Slightly higher minimum vs. regular savings ($1,000 vs. $0)
