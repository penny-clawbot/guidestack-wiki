# How to Start Investing with $100

You can start investing with $100 by using low‑cost brokers that allow **fractional shares** and no minimums, such as Fidelity, Charles Schwab, or the micro‑investing app Acorns. A $100 lump‑sum invested in a diversified index fund earning the market’s long‑term average of about **7 % per year** will grow to roughly **$761 after 10 years**, and a systematic **$100 monthly contribution** will accumulate about **$122,000 over 30 years** (Source: Investor.gov compound‑interest calculator).

---

## Why $100 Is a Real Starting Point


![Hero image for how to start investing with 100 dollars](https://picsum.photos/seed/how-to-start-investing-with-100-dollars-hero/1200/630)


- **No‑minimum accounts are abundant.** In 2023, 68 % of U.S. brokerages surveyed by NerdWallet offered at least one account type with a **$0 minimum** (NerdWallet, 2023).
- **Micro‑investing apps democratize entry.** Apps like Acorns, Stash, and Robinhood let you invest spare change or exact amounts **as low as $1** per transaction.
- **Fractional shares make diversification possible.** With $100 you can buy a fraction of high‑price stocks such as Amazon (~$170 per share) or Tesla (~$250 per share). A $100 allocation can be split across **3–5 different assets**, spreading risk without needing a large lump sum.
- **Historical returns validate the strategy.** The S&P 500 index has returned roughly **7 % annually** after inflation over the past 90 years (Credit Suisse Global Investment Returns Yearbook 2023). Even a modest $100 can benefit from this compounding power.

## The Best Platforms for Investing $100

| Platform | Minimum Investment | Key Feature | Typical Expense Ratio |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Fidelity** | $0 | No‑commission ETF trades, fractional shares, automatic investing | ETFs start at **0.03 %** (e.g., Fidelity Zero Total Market ETF) |
| **Charles Schwab** | $0 | Schwab Intelligent Portfolios (robo‑advisor) with **no advisory fee** | ETFs average **0.06 %** |
| **Acorns** | $5 (first deposit) | “Round‑up” purchases, automatically invests spare change | **$3/month** flat fee (≈0.3 % on $1,000) |
| **Stash** | $5 (first deposit) | Thematic investing (e.g., “Clean Energy”), $2–$9 monthly subscription | ETFs ≈ 0.10 % + subscription |
| **M1 Finance** | $100 (for a Portfolio) | **Automatic rebalancing**, free fractional shares | No management fee, ETFs ≈ 0.10 % |
| **Robinhood** | $0 | Commission‑free stocks, ETFs, and crypto; **instant deposit** up to $1,000 | No extra fees, but no fractional shares for some assets |

**Key takeaways when choosing a platform:**

- **Low costs** – Prioritize brokers with $0 commissions and low expense‑ratio ETFs.
- **Automatic investing** – Look for recurring‑investment options to enforce discipline.
- **Fractional shares** – Essential for stretching $100 across multiple holdings.
